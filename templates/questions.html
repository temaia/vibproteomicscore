Data analysis
What is LFQ?

LFQ (LabelFree Quantification) intensity values are the normalized MS intensity across all samples. The normalization corrects for differences in total protein amounts between the samples. This makes it possible to compare the samples. The LFQ intensity values are the values that you should use for further analysis.

What is iBAQ?

The iBAQ ratio is the MS intensity corrected for the theoretical detectability of the protein. The higher the iBAQ ratio, the more abundant a protein is WITHIN a sample.

3. Put samples in tubes and label them well

Samples should be delivered in tubes (Eppendorf, 15 ml, 50 ml,...). Samples in plates will only be accepted when delivered in person and if they were not mixed/spilled during the delivery process.

Label your samples well, preferably with short names on stickers. Also provide a list of all samples to the VIB Proteomics Core.Place all samples in a sealable plastic (ziplock) bag or box before putting them in the dry ice and shipping container.

4. Label the shipping container and add extra info

Label the shipping container with:

  • The name of the lab
  • The name of the person sending the samples
  • the PRC number
